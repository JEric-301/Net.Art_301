Explanation

The correct answer is Vitamin D. While Vitamin D is often associated solely with

strong bones and calcium absorption, its role in the body is far more extensive.

It actually functions much like a hormone and is crucial for regulating hundreds of genes

that control cell growth, immune function, and reduction of inflammation. A unique

feature is that our bodies can synthesize Vitamin D when the skin is exposed to

sunlight, making it one of the few vitamins we don't rely completely on diet for, but

consistent sun exposure and dietary intake are both necessary to avoid deficiency and

ensure optimal body function.
