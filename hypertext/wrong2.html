Explanation

The correct answer is O- (O Negative) because this blood type lacks two major types of

identification tags (antigens): the A and B antigens and the Rh factor antigen.

Since O- blood has no antigens on its red blood cells, it is not recognized as foreign by the

recipient's immune system, regardless of their own blood type. This unique quality makes

O- the safest option for emergency transfusions when a patient's blood type is

unknown, earning it the title of "Universal Donor."
